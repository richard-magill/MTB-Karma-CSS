Project Brief: MTB Karma

Project Title & Description

MTB Karma. A website all about mountain biking, including pages devoted to favorite destinations, races and trails. It will have a blog page for tracking latest rides/trips, as well as a links page to take users to other resources like good shops and trail-building opportunities.

Business Needs

Business needs driving this project include the need for:

  • A rich resource for mountain bike race information
  • A site with fun and useful resources for MTB trip planning
  • A place where mountain bikers of all skill levels can go for inspiration and mojo

The revenue model for this product will be based on customer advertising. The site will include at least one advertising side banner.

Use Cases

Use cases include providing data for experienced riders looking for race information, trip planners seeking a great mountain biking destination, cycling enthusiasts seeking high quality photos or other inspiration, and anyone out to find a new trail.

Stakeholders & Users

Stakeholders will be a wide audience of mountain bikers, world-wide. The kind of person that might follow this could be someone looking for a new trail to try out, a place to visit, or a shop where they can buy a bike. The overall goal here is to provide a fun (and ultimately expandable) site that might get a rider on the trail, psyched about an event, or possibly linked to other cycling resources.

Project Requirements

The site will be structured (on initial release) to provide a minimum viable product with the following web pages:

  • Home Page
  • Top Destinations
  • Great Rides
  • Killer Races
  • Trip Blog
  • Links
The current proposed architecture would be to build the site as a client-server application, with a Java REST API (Spring Framework) as the back-end. The client side would be built with React.js. One of the key objectives will be to provide a responsive, intuitive, and beautifully designed interface.

Success Measures

Metrics would be based on three main elements:

  • Site usage data
  • Advertising revenue
  • User surveys

Competitors

Of course there are many competitors in this space, most notably Trailforks and MTB Project. Both of these sites offer extraordinarily good trail detail and maps. Another strength is functionality that integrates with other cycling apps such as Strava. A significant weakness for both is a lack of information regarding races. Additionally, neither site is particularly visually appealing or entertainment-oriented. While users would often go to those sites to get a map, neither site would be considered a place to just hang out or see what is happening.

Risks

Key risks include:

  • Gaining traction with users
  • Attracting enough advertisers to generate sufficient revenue
The main method to minimize risk will be by maintaining low overhead as the site is released. The site will grow organically through word of mouth and through promotional materials to be distributed at races and other cycling events.
